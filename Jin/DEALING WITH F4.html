How many times have you had this happen?

This is Jin’s f+4, CDS 1+2 and is possibly one of the most solid frame traps in the game. It cannot be parried, or even powered through with armor, and it has ONE specific timing for a duck, meaning that you can only do this on a solid read. This is because CDS 1+2 is a two hit string, meaning that if you try to fuzzy guard, you will get up and block the second hit; leaving Jin safe.

This is only on hit. If you block Jins f+4 and he attempts to go into CDS (Crouching Demon Stance), this means that he is at -1. The slowest move that you can use to interrupt is i13 frames. Your best option is almost ALWAYS do to do a generic d/f+1 if you have one.

The reason being is that:

  • It is safe
  • It is a mid (meaning CDS 4 and staying in CDS cannot high crush)
  • It stops all other options

If your character has a generic d/f+4 that will push Jin out (for example Asukas d/f+4), it is advisable to use that instead. This is so Jin doesn’t use your d/f+1 to inadvertently steal a turn from you - such as in a Jin mirror match, because Jins own d/f+1 is -3. This would mean a Jin auto piloting d/f+1 in this situation is giving away a free turn if the Jin player does indeed cancel their CDS into a block.

Alternatively, if you feel like you have a read, you can SSR (side step right) and easily get into his back. This will not account for Jin CDS cancelling, he will simply re-align, and your opportunity will be lost.
